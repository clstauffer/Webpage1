ABOUT ME

My name is Catherine Stauffer and I am a graduate research assistant and Ph.D. candidate under the advisement of {Dr. Allison Wing}. I have lived in Tallahassee for ten years now but lived in Connecticut, Colorado, and New Mexico before that as well as having family in Nebraska where I have spent many a holiday.

I obtained my Bachelor's of Science in Meteorology from {FSU} in 2018 where I graduated *cum laude*, {with honors}, and as a {Garnet and Gold Scholar}. My honors thesis was under the advisement of {Dr. Mark Bourassa} and titled {Air-Sea Interaction as Observed by Research Vessel Data}. During my undergraduate career I was also a meteorological analyst for {RMS H*WIND} where I worked 24/7 operational shifts during the 2016, 2017, and part of the 2018 hurricane season. With RMS I participated in the {FSU UROP} program creating reanalyses of 1{980 Hurricane Allen} and presented a poster on the {maximum potential intensities of 2017 hurricanes} at the {2018 AMS Tropical Meteorology and Hurricanes Conference}.

While taking my Ph.D. preliminary exams I completed an additional day of testing to obtain a Master's of Science in Meteorology in 2020. As a part of this M.S. route I also wrote a small paper and presented it to a M.S. committee. You can read the paper {here} and view the powerpoint {here}.

I am working on the {Radiative-Convective Equilibrium Model Intercomparison Project}
